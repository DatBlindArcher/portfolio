Robbe Decraemer

a.k.a. Blind Archer

Self-Taught

My journey began from when I was at a very young age, fiddling small components together in GameMaker Studio. I created little puzzle platforms and was thrilled when my classmates tested them out.

But I wanted bigger challenges, larger games and made a move to Unity. Following Youtube tutorials and working together with people online that shared the same interest, taught me the C# language quickly.

After working and learning on my own solo projects, I started looking for teams to work together on something. And soon enough, I've worked on several projects together with people all around the world.

Passion for Game Development

Most of my time was spent playing games. Gaming interested me in a way that I wanted to learn what is all behind it. So naturally, I looked up how games were made, it all looked very complicated and a huge amount of work.

Nonetheless, there was hope. There were a few tutorials on how to start with something small. Since then I kept learning more and more about game development. Even now, many years later, I'm still finding something new to take on every day.

Today, I find myself tackling topics like 3D Game Engine development and XR applications.

Simulation Project
Suicide Prevention is a project that consists of two educational story based games featuring a mostly linear story structure. One is a 2D-game made for the browser, the other is a VR tool with 360 recordings. Students can use this tool to experience what decisions a healthcare worker could come across to prepare and prevent possible suicides.
